Good Positioning For BreastFeeding

Good Position

Is the infant correctly positioned? Positioning refers to how the baby is:
1. Infant's head and body straight
2. Infant facing the mother with the nose opposite the nipple
3. Infant's body close to the mother's body (infant's tummy to Mother's tummy)
4. Mother supporting infant's whole body and not just neck and shoulder.

All the 4 signs of correct positioning must be present to decide there is correct positioning.

Good Attachment

Is the infant able to attach? To check for attachment look for:
1. Chin touching the breast
2. Mouth wide open
3. Lower lip turned outward
4. More areola seen above than below the mouth.

Again all the 4 signs of good attachment must be present for one to decide the infant has good attachment

Signs of Effective Suckling:

1. Slow deep sucks, sometimes pausing
2. Chicks round when suckling
3. Baby releases breast when milk is finished or he/she is satisfied
4. Mother supporting infant's whole body and not just neck and shoulders.

How to attach:

1. Touch the baby's upper lip with your nipple
2. Wait until the baby's mouth is open wide
3. Move the baby quickly onto your breast, aiming the baby's lower lip well below the nipple
4. Mother feels relaxed
